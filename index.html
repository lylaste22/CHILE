CHILE 2015

Chile-Harvard Innovative Learning Exchange 2015

About

CHILE 2015 is a joint research effort between the Institute for Applied Computational Science (IACS) at Harvard University and the Center for Mathematical Modeling (CMM) at the Universidad de Chile. From Jan 4-17, 2015, students from each university worked in three teams to address real-world computational challenges drawn from a cutting-edge astronomical endeavor, the Dark Energy Camera.

Data

The data for this project included CCD images from the Victor M. Blanco Telescope’s Dark Energy Camera (DECam) at the Cerro Tololo Inter-American Observatory.

  • forty fields observed, during four nights, four times per nights, whith g filter, every image covers 3 sq. deg.
  • aprox 2 TBytes of data in FITS format.

Objective

Develop fast, cost-effective computational methods to process and analyze DECam data and provide recommendations for followup observations.

Group Tasks

  1. Process raw CCD images - Identify sources and produce light curves.
  2. Classify variable stars from light curves into known groups.
  3. Recommend time and targets for future observations to enhance understanding.
